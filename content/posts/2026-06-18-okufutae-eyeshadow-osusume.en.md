"Even with eyeshadow, the color hides in the crease," "By evening, color pools in the crease and creases up"—these are eye-makeup worries unique to hooded (inner-double) eyes.

Once you grasp **color choice and application tips**, hooded eyes can look bigger both vertically and horizontally. In this article, we present **recommended eyeshadows for hooded eyes** in a ranking, along with how to choose.

## 3 Points for Hooded Eyes Choosing Eyeshadow

### 1. Choose a crease-resistant "high-adherence type"

Hooded eyes have a narrow crease, so color pools easily with blinking. Choosing a **high-adherence, crease-resistant eyeshadow** keeps eyes clean until evening.

### 2. Add "vertical height" with a deepening shade

Since color hides in the crease, working a **well-pigmented deepening shade** at the lash line tightens the eye and adds vertical height. Brown or smoky shades are recommended.

### 3. Keep glitter restrained—fine pearl

Large glitter tends to emphasize lid thickness. For hooded eyes, choose **fine pearl** and use it as an accent above the iris or on the lower lash line—not over the whole lid—to avoid puffiness.

## Eyeshadow Application Tips for Hooded Eyes

- **Apply the deepening shade while checking with eyes open**: Find the range that doesn't hide in the crease
- **A little above the socket**: Spreading bright color above the crease makes eyes look taller
- **Extend the deepening shade toward the outer corner**: Adds width for a balanced look
- **Prep a thin base**: Using an eyeshadow base improves color hold and prevents creasing
