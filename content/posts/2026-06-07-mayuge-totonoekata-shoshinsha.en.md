## Tools & Prep You'll Need

First, gather your tools. To learn how to shape your brows, the following minimum items are enough.

| Item | Use |
|---|---|
| Brow scissors | Trim brow length |
| Brow comb (spoolie) | Comb the hairs / set long ones |
| Tweezers or brow razor | Remove unwanted hairs |
| Brow pencil / powder | Draw in the shape |
| Cotton swabs & concealer | Fix mistakes |

The best time to shape brows is **right after a bath**. Your pores are open, making hairs easier to remove and trim. Working under a bright light also makes fine hairs easier to see.

---

## Step 1: Picture Your "Ideal Shape"

Plucking or cutting right away is risky. Start by **imagining the brow shape that suits you**.

Remember these **3 key points** to make shaping easier:

1. **Brow head**: Aligns with an imaginary line up from the side of your nostril. Don't pluck hairs inside this point.
2. **Brow peak**: On the line extended from the outer edge of your iris. This is the highest point.
3. **Brow tail**: On the line connecting your nostril and the outer corner of your eye. End naturally beyond this point.

Marking the 3 points with concealer or a brow pencil before shaping makes it easier to keep both brows even.

---

## Step 2: Trim the Length (Scissors + Comb)

Before plucking, **trim the length first**. Long hairs often make brows look unkempt, so doing this carefully alone changes the impression a lot.

### How to do it

1. Comb the hairs upward with the brow comb
2. Trim hairs that stick out past the comb
3. Next, comb downward and trim the protruding hairs the same way
4. Check the overall balance

**Tip**: Don't try to cut a lot at once—trim little by little to avoid mistakes. Cutting too much can't be fixed until it regrows, so be careful.

---

## Step 3: Remove Unwanted Hairs (Tweezers or Razor)

Once the shape is set, remove the hairs that stray outside the brow.

### Using tweezers

- Pull from the root, following the direction of growth
- Don't yank a bunch at once—do it one hair at a time
- Step back and check the whole brow as you go to avoid over-plucking

### Using a brow razor (shaver)

- Less painful than tweezers, great for beginners
- Gently pull the skin taut and glide as if shaving off one thin layer
- Removes fine vellus hairs too, leaving the brow area neat

After removing hairs, soothe your skin with **toner or emulsion**. Open pores invite bacteria, so moisturizing is a must.

---

## Common Mistakes and Fixes

### Mistake 1: Over-plucked the brow head

Sparse brow heads make the whole face look vague. **Draw the hairs back in one by one with a brow pencil** for a natural fix.

### Mistake 2: The two brows ended up uneven

If the peaks differ left to right, the face can look lopsided. Move the mirror back to see the whole picture, or take a selfie with your phone's front camera to spot the difference.

### Mistake 3: Brows got too thin

Overly thin brows can look aged or unnatural. The trendy "natural brow" is on the thicker side, making the most of the hair flow. If you've over-plucked, fill in with brow powder as a soft block of color.

## Summary: Shape Your Brows in 3 Steps

Mastering how to shape your eyebrows dramatically raises the polish of your whole look.

1. **Picture your ideal shape** (set the 3 points: head, peak, tail)
2. **Even out the length** (trim protruding hairs with comb + scissors)
3. **Remove unwanted hairs** (tidy the outline with tweezers or a razor)

It's okay if it doesn't go perfectly at first—your brows will always grow back. Just giving it a try is the fastest route to improvement. Gather your tools and take your time in front of the mirror.
