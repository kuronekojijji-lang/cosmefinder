"Brow powder" creates soft, natural brows. But when choosing, you may wonder, "What color do I pick?" or "How many shades in the palette?"

In this article, we clearly explain **how to choose brow powder**, with points on color, number of shades, and texture. We also share recommended items and usage tips, so use it as a reference to find your perfect one.

## 3 Points for Choosing Brow Powder

### 1. Choose color to match your hair

The basics of brow color are **the same as or slightly lighter than your hair**. Light hair pairs with light brown, dark hair with gray or ash tones. To keep brows from standing out, choosing by your hair color is the foolproof key.

### 2. Choose by number of shades (gradient)

With a **palette of around 3 shades**, you can use a light shade at the head and a dark shade at the tail to build a gradient. For dimensional, natural brows, a multi-shade set is by far recommended.

### 3. Check texture and powder quality

Powder that's too hard barely shows, while too soft scatters easily. Choosing a **powder quality that adheres to skin and resists smudging** keeps brows clean until evening.

## Brow Powder Usage Tips

- **Light shade at the head, dark shade at the tail**: A natural gradient creates dimension
- **Layer a little at a time**: Don't go dark at once—apply thin then adjust
- **Blend with a spoolie**: Tidying the hair flow while blending gives a natural finish
- **Keep the brow head especially light**: A dark head looks unnatural, so soft is best
