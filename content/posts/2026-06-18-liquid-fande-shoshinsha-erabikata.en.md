"I want to try a liquid foundation, but there are so many types I don't know how to choose," "How do I pick color and finish?"—choosing your first liquid foundation is full of uncertainty.

In this article, we clearly explain **how to choose a liquid foundation for beginners**, on three axes: color, finish, and skin type. We also share recommended items and application tips, so use it as a reference to find the one that suits you.

## 3 Points for Choosing a Liquid Foundation

### 1. Choose a type that suits your skin

First, choose by your skin type. **Dry skin: moist/dewy types**; **oily skin: oil-resistant semi-matte types** that resist breakdown. For combination skin, using different types on different areas works too.

### 2. Choose by finish (glow / matte)

A **glow finish** gives a healthy, hydrated impression; a **matte finish** gives a polished, composed impression. Choosing by the image you want reduces mistakes. For beginners, a natural glow type is easy to handle and recommended.

### 3. Choose a color that suits your skin tone

For color, **one close to your neck or jawline** blends naturally. Beware overly bright colors that lift. If unsure, test a little on your jawline in store.

## Basics of Applying Liquid Foundation

- **Place small amounts at a few spots**: Place a pearl-size dot on the forehead, both cheeks, nose, and chin, then spread
- **From the face center outward**: Thicker inside, thinner outside for natural dimension
- **Press with a sponge**: After spreading by hand, lightly press with a sponge to resist breakdown
- **Layer from thin**: Don't apply all at once—layer only where you want coverage
