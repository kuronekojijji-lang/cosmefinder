The number-one cause of failure when choosing eyeshadow for natural makeup is "color that lifts." **Choosing a flattering color and the right texture is the key to success.**

---

## Natural Makeup Eyeshadow: 3 Basics

### ① Choose brown / beige

The basics of natural makeup are **using eyeshadow to create a "bare-skin feel."** For that:

- Main color: beige to light brown
- Deepening shade: a deep brown (in place of eyeliner)
- Highlight: a champagne shimmer

With these 3 colors, you can create natural dimension.

### ② Texture: shimmer to semi-matte

A single matte is hard for beginners, while a **shimmer-pearl type** gives natural sheen and looks well done.

### ③ Start with a 4-to-5-shade palette

Rather than combining several singles, **a pre-set palette** is harder to mess up thanks to its color balance.

## How to Apply Natural Makeup: 3 Steps

The trick to looking natural is **layering thinly**.

1. Lightly apply the **base color (beige)** over the whole lid with a brush
2. Apply the **medium color (brown)** a little wider than the crease
3. Apply the **deepening shade (dark brown)** at the lash line in place of eyeliner

Applying with your **finger** rather than a tip gives a more natural finish.
