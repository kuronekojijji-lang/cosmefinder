## Basics to Know Before Applying Blush

The role of blush is to add **flush and dimension** to the face. Placement and shape change the impression a lot.

Beginners should start with **cream blush**, which adheres to skin and is hard to mess up, or **powder blush**, which applies softly.

## How to Apply Blush [3 Steps]

### Step 1: Decide the placement

The basic natural placement is the **part of the cheek that rises when you smile**.

- Center it around where the line below your iris meets the line out from your nostril
- Smile and find where the cheek is highest
- Aim for one to two finger-widths below the under-eye (too close looks puffy)

### Step 2: Take a small amount and apply

The rule for blush is **a little at a time**. Taking a lot at once makes it too heavy.

- For powder, take it on a brush and tap off the excess on a tissue once
- For cream, take just a little on your fingertip
- Place it gently at the center of the cheek

### Step 3: Blend toward the outside

Softly blend the applied blush **from the cheek center toward the outside and upward**. Lightly blending so the edges disappear gives a natural flush.

## Common Mistakes and Fixes

| Common mistake | Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Too heavy | Took too much at once | Apply a little at a time, layer thinly |
| Tomboyish / childish | Placement too low & too round | Apply as an oval slightly toward the outside |
| Looks puffy | Too close to the eye | Drop 1–2 finger-widths below the under-eye |
| Crisp edges | Not blended enough | Blend with a clean brush |

## Tips by Face Shape

- **Round face**: Apply diagonally (up and out) along the cheekbone for a sharper impression
- **Long face**: Apply as a horizontal oval at the cheek center to soften the length
- **Square face**: Apply roundly at the high point of the cheek for a softer impression

## Summary

The basics of applying blush are 3 steps: "① decide placement → ② take a small amount and apply → ③ blend toward the outside." Centering on the cheek's high point when you smile, keeping it away from the eye, and applying a little at a time is the foolproof key to a natural flush.

Beginners should start with skin-adhering cream blush or softly pigmented powder blush. Mind the placement for your face shape, too, and enjoy a healthy, bright, flushed face.
