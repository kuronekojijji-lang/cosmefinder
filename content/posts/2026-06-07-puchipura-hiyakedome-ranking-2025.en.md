## 3 Points for Choosing an Affordable Sunscreen

### 1. Choose by SPF / PA value

The basics of sunscreen come down to checking SPF and PA.

| Scenario | Recommended value |
|---|---|
| Indoors / daily | SPF20–30 / PA++ |
| Commuting / shopping | SPF30–50 / PA+++ |
| Outdoors / sports | SPF50+ / PA++++ |

For daily use, choosing **SPF50 / PA++++** covers every scenario. Many affordable products claim the highest values, so there's no need to compromise.

### 2. Choose by texture and finish

- **Lightweight type**: Best for combination/oily skin bothered by oil. Non-sticky and crease-resistant
- **Moisturizing type**: For dry or sensitive skin. Hydrating ingredients give a moist finish
- **Tone-up type**: Clears dullness for a brighter look—can also be used as a primer

### 3. Choose by whether it's waterproof

For summer or outdoor activities where sweat and water are a concern, a **waterproof type** is reassuring. Note that it requires cleansing, so choose based on your lifestyle.

- **Pure value focus** → CEZANNE (¥550)

The key with sunscreen is making it a daily habit. Affordable ones let you use them generously—apply plenty not just to your face but to your neck and the backs of your hands. Find your favorite and make daily UV care a habit.
