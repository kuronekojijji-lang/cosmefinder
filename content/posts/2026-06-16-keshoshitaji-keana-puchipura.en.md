"Even with foundation, my pores still show as little dots." "By evening, my makeup looks messy from pores." Such pore troubles can actually change a lot with your **choice of primer**.

In this article, we hand-pick and rank **affordable primers that cover pores**. We also explain how to choose and tips for application, so if you're aiming for smooth, pore-less skin, please use it as a reference.

## 3 Points for Choosing a Pore-Covering Primer

### 1. A "pore-blurring formula" that fills pore texture

If you're bothered by enlarged pores and texture, choose a primer with a **pore-blurring formula that smooths pores flat**. It diffuses light to make pore shadows less visible.

### 2. A type that prevents oil-related "pore slipping"

For those whose makeup slips into pores over time due to oil, an **oil-controlling primer** is recommended. It curbs shine and keeps skin smooth.

### 3. Even affordable, choose by adherence

Even an inexpensive primer with high adherence dramatically improves foundation wear. There are plenty of excellent items even around **¥1,000**.

## How to Apply Primer So Pores Don't Show

- **A small amount, thinly**: Heavy application causes pore slipping—place a rice-grain amount at a few spots and spread
- **Against the direction of pores**: On pore areas, work bottom-up in small circles to fill them
- **Press with a sponge**: After applying by hand, lightly press with a sponge to boost adherence
