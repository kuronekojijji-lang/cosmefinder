"I finished my makeup nicely in the morning, but by evening it's a greasy mess," "Foundation sticks to my mask"—many struggle with makeup breakdown when they want to keep clean skin even at work.

## 3 Points for Choosing a Long-Lasting Foundation

### 1. A type that controls oil

The main cause of breakdown is oil. Choosing a foundation or powder with an **oil-absorbing, oil-controlling formula** prevents shine and pore slipping, keeping you clean for hours.

### 2. Check the adherence

A foundation that clings closely to skin resists breakdown. Choose a **high-fit liquid or powder** and finish thinly and evenly to improve wear.

### 3. Boost "hold" with finishing powder

Beyond foundation alone, layering a **finishing face powder** dramatically improves crease resistance. There are many excellent powders even among affordable options.

## Tips to Keep Foundation From Breaking Down

- **3 stages: primer → foundation → powder**: Layering dramatically improves crease resistance
- **Wait a bit after skincare**: Applying while skin is oily and sticky leads to breakdown
- **Thin on oil-prone areas**: Don't apply the T-zone thickly—control it with powder
- **Fix with blotting paper + powder**: When it breaks down, blot the oil then layer powder
