## Lip Monster Shades by Type

### Red

A classic color that's glamorous and instantly brightens the complexion. Having one shines for polished makeup too. Blue-reds suit cool tones, orange-reds suit warm tones.

### Brown / terracotta

An effortless, mature impression. On-trend and especially flattering for warm tones. A versatile color usable for casual or clean looks.

### Pink / rose

A soft, feminine impression. It adds flush and suits cool tones. An easy color for daily use.

### Burgundy / muted

A deep, mature impression. Perfect for autumn/winter makeup or a mode vibe. Recommended when you want individuality.

## Recommended by Skin Tone (Personal Color)

| Skin type | Recommended family | Impression |
|---|---|---|
| Warm Spring | Orange-red, coral | Bright, fresh |
| Warm Autumn | Terracotta, brown | Effortless, mature |
| Cool Summer | Rose, pink | Gentle, elegant |
| Cool Winter | Blue-red, burgundy | Glamorous, crisp |

If unsure, start with the flattering **terracotta or rose** family.

## Tips for Using Lip Monster Well

- **Moisturize with a lip balm first**: Even matte gets less drying
- **Layer thinly to adjust**: Highly pigmented, so build from thin to your preferred intensity
- **Blur the outline**: Blending with a finger looks contemporary and effortless
- **Tissue-off to boost hold**: Blotting once and reapplying makes it adhere

## Summary

The key to choosing Lip Monster shades is to choose by **your personal color and desired impression**.

For glamour, red; for effortlessness, terracotta or brown; for gentleness, pink or rose; for individualists, burgundy. If unsure, starting with the flattering terracotta or rose is hard to get wrong. Don't forget to moisturize before applying, and enjoy a fade-resistant, highly pigmented lip.
