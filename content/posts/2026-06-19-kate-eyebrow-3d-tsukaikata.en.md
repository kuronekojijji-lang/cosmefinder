## How to Use the 3 Shades

The 3 shades of Designing Eyebrow 3D each have different roles.

- **The lightest shade**: For the brow head and the whole-brow base. Soft and bright
- **The middle shade**: The main color to fill the whole brow
- **The darkest shade**: To define the brow tail and outline

Combining these 3 shades creates natural light-and-dark on a brow that tends to look flat.

## How to Use KATE Designing Eyebrow 3D [Steps]

### Step 1: Fill the whole brow with the middle shade

First, take the middle shade on the included brush and softly fill the whole brow. Keep the head light and go slightly darker toward the tail.

### Step 2: Define the tail and outline with the dark shade

With the darkest shade, fill the brow tail and lower brow line. Refining the outline creates dimension and definition.

### Step 3: Blur the head with the light shade

Softly place the lightest shade on the brow head and blur with a spoolie. The head blends naturally for a soft impression.

### Step 4: Add nose shadow

Sweeping the leftover light or middle shade from the brow head toward the nose bridge gives a slimmer look with natural shadow.

## Tips for a Beautiful Finish

- **Don't darken the brow head**: A light shade, softly, is the rule
- **Layer a little at a time**: Don't go dark at once—apply thin and adjust
- **Blur with a spoolie**: Tidying the hair flow while blending looks natural
- **Keep nose shadow light**: Too much looks unnatural, so be subtle
