## What to Prepare Before Applying Eyeshadow

First, gather what you need. Beginners should start with a palette-type eyeshadow. Since several shades come together, gradients are easy to build, and many include a usage guide.

**What you need**

- Eyeshadow palette (ideally a 4+ shade gradient palette)
- Tip (the one included, or your finger)
- Eyeshadow brush (handy to have)
- Eyeshadow base (if you're concerned about creasing)

**Basic shade structure of an eyeshadow**

A typical 4-shade palette is made up of these roles.

| Shade brightness | Role | Where to apply |
|---|---|---|
| Highlight (brightest) | Adds dimension | Base over the whole lid |
| Medium | Creates natural shadow | Crease or lash line |
| Shading (darker) | Depth and definition | Lash line / outer corner |
| Glitter | Adds glamour | Accent on inner corner or center |

## How to Apply Eyeshadow [3 Steps for Beginners]

### Step 1: Even out the whole lid with the highlight shade

First, spread the **brightest shade (highlight)** thinly over the entire lid. Using a finger or tip, gently stamp it on from the inner to the outer corner.

**Tips**
- Don't press hard—gently pat it on
- Applying it evenly over the whole lid helps later shades blend
- At this stage, even too-light is fine

For beginners, using a finger gives better adherence and is harder to mess up, so it's recommended.

### Step 2: Add the medium shade to the crease or lash line

Next, add the **medium shade** to the crease or lash line. This is the key spot for the gradient.

**For double eyelids**
- Apply along the crease, from the lash line up a little above
- Going slightly wider toward the outer corner makes eyes look bigger

**For monolids / hooded lids**
- Apply within the narrow visible width (2–3 mm) when eyes are open
- Aim for the width that shows color when you squint

**Using a brush**
A brush makes blending easier. After applying color, blend the line with a clean brush or finger for a natural gradient.

### Step 3: Define the eyes with the shading shade

Finally, add the **darkest shade (shading)** to the lash line and outer corner. This brings depth and completes the gradient.

**Applying along the lash line**
- With a tip or thin brush, draw a fine line along the lash roots
- Widening it toward the outer corner makes eyes look bigger

**Outer-corner shading**
- From about the outer third, draw a triangle toward the outside
- Stop before it looks "too crisp," then blend with a finger for a natural finish

**Glitter accent (optional)**
If you have time, placing glitter on the inner corner or above the iris adds glamour. Just pat on a small amount with a fingertip.

## Common Mistakes and Fixes

### Mistake 1: Color looks muddy and messy

**Cause**: Too much dark shade, or not enough blending
**Fix**: When using dark shades, apply a little at a time and carefully blend the edges with a brush or finger.

### Mistake 2: It creases or fades over time

**Cause**: The shadow lifts due to lid oil
**Fix**: Applying an eyeshadow base beforehand greatly improves wear. Without a base, even a thin layer of concealer on the lid helps.

### Mistake 3: Eyes look puffy

**Cause**: Applying dark shades over the whole lid
**Fix**: Keep dark shades within the lash line to crease range, and apply only bright shades over the whole lid.

### Mistake 4: The two eyes turn out different

**Cause**: Drawing with your non-dominant hand is hard
**Fix**: Place the mirror at eye level and check both eyes at once for better balance.

---

## Pro Tips: Techniques for an Even Prettier Look

### Add color to the lower lash line

Adding a matching shade to the outer part of the lower lash line tightens the whole eye. But too much looks heavy, so apply thinly and finely.

### Place highlight on the inner corner

A highlight or glitter accent on the inner corner makes eyes look wider.

### Finish with a setting mist

Spraying a setting mist over the whole face after makeup prevents creasing and improves wear.

## Summary: 3 Steps to Apply Eyeshadow

Let's recap the **beginner eyeshadow application** introduced here.

1. **Even out the whole lid with the highlight shade** — spread thinly and evenly to build a base
2. **Add the medium shade to the crease / lash line** — the key step for the gradient
3. **Define the eyes with the shading shade** — apply finely to the outer corner and lash line, then blend

It's okay if it doesn't go well at first. Makeup improves with practice. Repeat the simple 3 steps and learn the quirks of your own lids.
