## What Are Straight Brows? Why the Trend Lasts

Straight brows are drawn with little arch, keeping the brow nearly horizontal from head to tail. Compared to arched brows, they reduce the vertical space on the face, creating a **soft, approachable impression**.

The trend lasts because of its perfect balance—looking effortless without seeming too youthful. They suit both natural and trendy makeup and are embraced across generations.

## How to Draw Straight Brows [3 Steps]

### Step 1: Set the guidelines

The most important part of straight brows is **positioning the head, peak, and tail**.

- **Brow head**: Directly above the base of your nostril
- **Brow tail**: On the line connecting your nostril and the outer corner of your eye
- **Brow peak**: Around the outer edge of the iris to just before the tail (kept subtle for straight brows)

Keeping the head and tail at "nearly the same height" makes a horizontal line easy to create.

### Step 2: Build a base with powder

After tidying the hair flow with a spoolie, softly fill the whole brow with powder. Keep the head light and go slightly darker toward the tail.

Since straight brows don't emphasize the peak, apply powder while **keeping the top line horizontal**. The key is not creating an arch.

### Step 3: Refine the outline with pencil

Finally, fill gaps and the lower brow line hair by hair with a pencil. Keeping the underside horizontal makes the straight, level impression stand out.

## Common Mistakes and Fixes

Here are the points people stumble on with straight brows, and how to fix them.

| Common mistake | Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Brows end up uneven | Looking in the mirror too close | Step back and check the overall balance |
| Looks unnaturally straight | Drawing the underside dead straight | Leave a slight natural curve near the head |
| Too dark | Too much powder | Tap the color off the brush once on a tissue |
| Face looks harsh | Color too dark | Choose a shade slightly lighter than your hair |

## Tips to Make Straight Brows Look Great

- **Match the color to your hair**: Light hair pairs with light brown, dark hair with gray tones
- **Finish with brow mascara**: Tidying the hair flow adds dimension and refinement
- **Check from afar now and then**: Left-right balance is easier to judge at a distance

## Summary

The basics of straight brows are 3 steps: "① set the guidelines → ② build a base with powder → ③ refine the outline with pencil." Keeping the head and tail at nearly the same height and not emphasizing the peak are the keys to soft, polished straight brows.

Even if they don't match at first, adjusting while viewing the whole from a distance will help you get the balance. Start with easy-to-use powder + pencil items and enjoy creating your own straight brows.
