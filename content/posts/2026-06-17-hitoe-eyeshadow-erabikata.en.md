"Even with eyeshadow, the color doesn't show," "It makes my eyes look puffy"—these are eye-makeup worries unique to monolids. In fact, once you grasp **how to choose and apply eyeshadow**, monolids can look crisp and bigger.

In this article, we clearly explain **how to choose eyeshadow for monolids**, with color, texture, and application points. We also introduce recommended items, so if you want to show off your eyes at their best, please use it as a reference.

## 3 Points for Monolids Choosing Eyeshadow

### 1. Choose a palette with a clear deepening shade

Since monolids are flat and color shows less, a palette with a **well-pigmented deepening shade** is recommended. Adding dark color at the lash line makes the eyes look more defined.

### 2. Texture is basically "matte to fine pearl"

Large glitter or sparkly pearl tends to emphasize lid thickness and look puffy. For monolids, choosing **matte or fine pearl** gives crisp, defined eyes.

### 3. Define with "brown / smoky" colors

Rather than overly bright colors that look expanding, **defining colors like brown and smoky** suit monolids. They create natural shadow and dimension on the eyes.

## Eyeshadow Application Tips for Monolids

- **Make the deepening shade wider**: Since color hides easily when monolids open, apply the lash-line deepening shade a bit wider
- **Aim for horizontal width**: Extending the deepening shade toward the outer corner adds width to look bigger
- **Glitter on the aegyo-sal or under the iris**: Rather than large glitter on the lid, using it on the lower lash line prevents puffiness
- **Unify with the eyeliner**: Blending the deepening shade with the liner makes the eyes more defined
